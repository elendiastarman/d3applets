Taxonomy of Game Mechanics

An ongoing attempt to boil down game mechanics to their core fundamentals.

This taxonomy is patterned after the taxonomic ranks used in biology: domain, kingdom, phylum, class, family, genus, species, in order from largest and most inclusive to smallest and narrowest. Independent attributes are colored to set them apart.

[Expand all]

  • Space – a collection of game states Note: exceptions to the independence of attributes are noted in [brackets].
    • Layout – how the coordinates of the space relate to one another
      • Grid-based – coordinates are located relative to each other in a regular, repeated pattern
        • Linear – equivalent to the number line
        • Rectangular/cubical – two or more orthogonal axes, set at 90 degrees relative to each other
        • Hexagonal – two axes, where each coordinate is "adjacent" to three others
        • Triangular – two axes, where each coordinate is "adjacent" to six others
        • Other – two or more axes, based on some tesselation or tiling
      • Network [discrete] – no regular pattern in the adjacency information
    • Granularity – the level of detail present in the coordinate system
      • Discrete – there is a clear discontinuity between two coordinates as close together as possible
      • Continuous – for every point P, there is at least one other point in an arbitrarily small neighborhood centered on P
    • Connectedness – the global nature
      • Without holes [grid-based] – All points that are in the space and within the boundary (if it exists) are playable
      • With holes [grid-based] – At least one point that is in the space and within the boundary (if it exists) is not playable
      • Disjoint – Two or more areas cannot be reached solely by moving between adjacent points
    • Bounds – the limitations (or lack thereof) on the playable portion of the space
      • Bounded – the playable area is finite
      • Partially bounded – the playable area is infinite in one or more directions
      • Toroidal – areas adjacent to the boundary are joined to non-adjacent areas
      • Unbounded – the playable area is infinite in all directions
    • Curvature [grid-based] – referring to Gaussian curvature
      • Euclidean – zero curvature, flat like a sheet of paper; the most common type
      • Elliptical – positive curvature, round like a ball
      • Hyperbolic – negative curvature like a saddle; hard to visualize, very rare
      • Other – some combination of the above, like a torus, which has both positive and negative curvature
  • Actor – an entity that performs actions
    • Decision-making process – how the actor makes decisions
      • Rational – an entity that chooses actions based on the information available to it
        • Sentient – an entity that acts toward the goal(s) based on the information available to it
          • Human – A human player
          • Computer – A computer/AI player of any level of intelligence
        • Dumb – an entity that does not actively work towards any goal
      • Irrational – an entity that performs a set, repeated pattern of actions
      • Random – a random-number generator
    • Friendliness – the relation of one actor towards another insofar as pursuing the same goal
      • Allied – helps the actor
      • Neutral – neither helps nor hurts the actor
      • Hostile – hurts the actor
      • Varied – sometimes helps or hurts the actor
  • Action – causes one or more transitions between game states
  • Transition – a rule determining how to get from one game state to another
    • Movement – an actor goes from one game space coordinate to another
      • Continuity – whether the path taken has gaps
        • Connected – each coordinate visited is "adjacent" to the previous and next ones
        • Disconnected – there are "jumps" between non-adjacent coordinates
      • Range – how far apart the initial and ending coordinates can be
        • Bounded – distance of travel in any direction is finite
        • Semi-bounded – distance of travel in at least one direction, but not all, is infinite
        • Unbounded – distance of travel in all directions is infinite
    • Speed – how quickly the transition happens
      • Instantaneous – happens immediately
      • Stepped – happens in increments
  • Interaction – a rule for how actors affect each other
  • Knowledge – the information available to an actor
  • Mystery – the information not available to an actor
    • Future actions – the actions that actors will make
    • Undiscovered – knowable in theory, but takes time and/or effort to discover
    • Hidden – unknowable unless revealed by another actor
  • Discovery – a rule determining how information moves from mystery to knowledge (or vice versa)
  • Goal – the aim of the game
    • Winning Method – how to determine the winner
      • First to finish – the winner achieves fulfills the win conditions first
      • Survivor – everyone else loses first
      • Situational – the winner is determined by the current/final game state
        • Endless – the game never ends and the winner may change at any time
        • Time limit – the game ends after a certain amount of time
        • Resource exhaustion – the game ends when it cannot be played any further
